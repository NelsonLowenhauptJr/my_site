about me

        
First the person, then the nerd.

      


      

        

          // personal
          

            Before any command line, I'm just a regular person. I have a family I love, my partner and
            Nero (our little dachshund). And that's who I spend most of my free time with. I study
            engineering, work with technology, and enjoy tinkering on a personal project just as much as a
            quiet day at home. No rush.
          

        


        

          // technology
          

            That said, the nerd here is the real deal. I've lived in the terminal since the days of the
            dearly missed Conectiva, when I learned to break (and fix) one install after another. I don't
            defend a distro: I             defend the whole Unix family. A garage-grown Linux distro or a hardcore BSD
            holding up a mission-critical system, if there's a prompt to call my own, I feel at home. My quirks have
            names: MATE on the desktop and good old mcedit as my editor. You don't abandon what's
            simple and gets the job done.
          

          

            I also have an undeclared life mission: getting Linux to run on things that were never meant to
            run Linux.             I've turned a forgotten ARMv7 TV box into a server and built an OpenWRT router on a
            device the project itself recommended against. And it worked. At home I keep a humble homelab
            and I like technology that turns into something useful: automation and home automation (the
            latest was putting the whole house on Zigbee) and a 3D printer I use to solve real problems.
            Not to decorate a shelf.
          

        


        

          // culture
          

            There's also the side that has nothing to do with computers. I'm a voracious comic book reader:
            Marvel for the heroes, DC for the villains. I grew up on classic X-Men, Fantastic Four,
            Spider-Man, Hulk, Punisher and Justice League. But I have a soft spot for the more grown-up
            stuff like Sandman, Preacher and Constantine. And I never forget the classics: The Phantom,
            Amigo da Onça, and Brazilian masters like Maurício de Sousa, Iotti and Verissimo's Analista de
            Bagé.
          

          

            I'm an unapologetic Sega fan and a member of the Mega Drive community. More than playing, I'm
            fascinated by how those consoles pulled off magic with such limited CPU, memory and storage. My
            shelf runs from a Nintendo 64 with an Everdrive to a Dreamcast, plus PSP, Wii, PS3 and an Xbox
            One VCR. And I emulate old hardware whenever I can (long live DOSBox). In film, I used to be a
            critic and I intend to go back to it; my favorite is "The Big Short", robbed at the Oscars. And
            I'm a card-carrying Trekker: I help run one of the largest Portuguese-language Star Trek groups,
            and I share my heart with Doctor Who and Stargate. Live long and prosper.
          

        

      

    


    

      

        
work

        
I'm one of the people who keep things running when it all hits the fan.

        

          My background is in technology, with plenty of mileage in development, financial institutions,
          automation and blockchain. Today I work as a DevOps Engineer, responsible for the infrastructure
          and cloud of a fintech focused on financial sovereignty and privacy. And I'm genuinely proud to be
          part of this team.
        

        

          And I'm happy doing it. I'm one of the people who keep important systems up, secure and under
          control: provisioning environments, automating deployments, monitoring what matters and putting
          out fires when they show up. I love it because I feel it makes a real difference in real people's
          lives. And it's exactly where I want to be.
